# Italy — direct-work-route factor scoring — 2026-06-19

**Profile:** Bangladeshi (Dhaka) couple, both ~2022 BSc-CS / software-AI engineers; goal migrate → PR → citizenship.

## Method
Gov-first WebSearch + WebFetch: European Commission EU Immigration Portal (Blue Card Italy), Ministero dell'Interno ALI/Sportello Unico portal, InfoMigrants + ILF/VisaHQ on Decreto Flussi 2026–2028, Ministry of Labour "Bangladeshi community in Italy" annual report, MarlowBray (Decreto Flussi vs Blue Card), Glassdoor/SalaryExpert (Milan SWE pay). ≥2 sources per decisive claim.

## Decisive findings
- **No blocker.** Italy allows dual citizenship without renunciation (Art. 11, Law 91/1992); Italy is on Bangladesh's DNC list → BD nationality retained (already `dual-citizenship-retention` 88).
- **The lever — EU Blue Card (Carta Blu UE) is QUOTA-EXEMPT.** Confirmed by EU Commission portal + MarlowBray + Jobbatical 2026: highly-qualified workers sit entirely outside the annual Decreto Flussi quota/click-day. An employer can sponsor a Dhaka engineer year-round.
- **Eligibility easily met:** recognised CS/IT degree (or 3 yrs relevant IT experience in prior 7) + 6-month+ contract above the salary floor. Floor ≈ 1.5× ISTAT average gross (~€35,000–38,300 in 2025–26), with a reduced ~1.2× (~€28,000) for ICT/shortage roles. Milan SWE pay (mid ~€42–62k, senior ~€83k) clears it comfortably.
- **Overseas direct-hire works:** employer files nulla osta at the Sportello Unico via the Interior Ministry ALI portal (~1–3 months); worker collects the visa at the consulate in Bangladesh; contratto di soggiorno signed within 8 days of arrival. Max ~90 days statutory, real-world 6.5–8 months incl. declaration-of-value.
- **Settlement chain:** Blue Card → EU long-term residence after 5 yrs EU (min 2 in Italy) → citizenship at **10 yrs + B1 Italian** (a June-2025 referendum to cut to 5 failed). Dual retained.

## Conflicts / caveats recorded
- **Salary threshold spread** across sources (€33,500 EU-Commission 2024 → €35k–38.3k 2026; ~€28k ICT-reduced). Resolved per protocol toward the most recent: ~1.5× ISTAT (~€35–38k) standard, ~1.2× (~€28k) ICT. Conclusion (engineer clears it) is robust to the spread.
- **Labour-market test:** EU Commission portal states Italy applies one for the Blue Card; firms report it is *often* waived for Blue Card. Treated as friction, not a bar → tempers `employer-sponsorship-willingness`.
- **Track-record confidence MODERATE.** The huge BD community (~150,692 official 2022; 175k–200k incl. undocumented; Bangladeshis now top-3 for new residence permits) arrived mainly via Decreto Flussi/family/asylum, NOT highly-qualified routes — Blue Card uptake is low (~747 issued *nationwide* in 2023). So "direct skilled-work track record" rests on community depth + residence-permit standing as proxies, with the Blue-Card-specific flow thin.
