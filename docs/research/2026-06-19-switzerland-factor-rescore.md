## Key decisive facts (cited in `switzerland.json`)

- **Citizenship (among the world's hardest):** 10 years' federal residence + cantonal/communal residence (2–5 yrs) + C permit + national language (B1 oral/A2 written) + integration + financial independence, decided commune→canton→confederation with local discretion. Henley 2026 joint **3rd (~186)**.
- **PSW (the weakest in the set):** only a **6-month, non-renewable** job-search permit (15 hrs/week); conversion to work is **non-EU quota-bound** with a labour-market test.
- **PR:** C permit generally needs **~10 years** for non-EU nationals.
- **Jobs:** the world's highest tech salaries (Google Zürich median ~CHF 292k; AI ~CHF 167–247k) and a premier AI hub (ETH, Google, IBM) — but quota-constrained for non-EU.
- **Study (a standout):** **ETH Zürich/EPFL, top-10 CS/AI, at ~CHF 1,460/yr** — world-class quality at near-free tuition.
- **Tax:** low (canton-dependent ~20–30% effective); Switzerland–Bangladesh treaty exists.
- **Healthcare:** world-class but via expensive mandatory private insurance (~CHF 300+/mo).
- **Safety:** very safe, top-tier rule of law (WJP top-10).
- **Politics/diaspora (the drag):** SVP (largest party) drives quota-based restriction and an anti-Islam streak — the **2009 minaret ban** and a **domestic halal-slaughter ban**; the Bangladeshi community is small.
